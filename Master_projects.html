Master Student projects

Molecular interactions and their role in biological systems are at the heart of our research activities. They play a fundamental role in many chemical and biological processes such as protein folding and function, aggregation, enzymatic reactions, ligand design, to name but a few. A deeper understanding of molecular interactions and the ability to make accurate computer models allows us to such problems at the atomic level. In our group we use state-of-the-art computational tools and construct model of complex system to investigate molecular interactions of proteins, antimicrobial peptides, lipid bilayers as well as small drug-like compounds.

Master students in our group have traditionally had a structural chemistry background with an interest to work the majority of their master project using computer modeling as their main tool.

Modeling of enzymatic reactions

Life has to effectively colonized most of our planet, and extremophilic organisms require specalized enzymes to survice under harsh conditions. We focus our attention to so-called cold-loving organisms (psychrophilic) that express heat-labile enzymes with high specific activity and catalytic activity at low to moderate temperatures.
